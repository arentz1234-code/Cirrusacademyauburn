Cirrus Airframe Parachute System

The Only Aircraft With
a Built-In Parachute.

CAPS has saved over 250 lives. It's the ultimate safety net — a whole-airframe ballistic parachute that brings the entire aircraft to the ground under a canopy in the event of an emergency.

1

Flight Training Programs

Your Path to the Skies.

Three structured programs. One incredible aircraft. Each rating builds on the last — from your first takeoff to flying commercially.

Private Pilot License (PPL)

Your journey begins here. Learn to fly the Cirrus SR20 and earn the freedom of flight. Our structured curriculum takes you from zero experience to confident, safe private pilot.

50-60 Flight Hours
4-6 Months
FAA Part 61
1

Discovery Flight

Experience the Cirrus SR20 firsthand with an introductory flight over Auburn.

Your first time in the left seat. You'll take the controls, experience the G1000 glass cockpit, and get a feel for what Cirrus flying is all about.

1.0 flight hour
2

Ground School & Written Exam Prep

Build the aeronautical knowledge foundation required by the FAA.

Comprehensive ground instruction covering aerodynamics, weather, regulations, navigation, and Cirrus-specific G1000 systems training.

40+ hours ground
3

Pre-Solo Flight Training

Master takeoffs, landings, maneuvers, and emergency procedures.

Dual instruction focusing on basic aircraft control, traffic patterns, slow flight, stalls, steep turns, and emergency procedures including CAPS decision-making.

15-20 flight hours
4

First Solo Flight

A milestone moment — your first flight as pilot in command.

After demonstrating proficiency, you'll take the SR20 around the pattern solo. One of aviation's greatest milestones — in a Cirrus.

Milestone event
5

Cross-Country Training

Learn navigation, flight planning, and G1000 GPS operations.

Fly to airports across Alabama. Master VOR/GPS navigation on the G1000 NXi, weather reading, fuel planning, and ATC communications.

10-15 flight hours
6

Solo Cross-Country Flights

Build confidence with solo flights to distant airports.

Complete FAA-required solo cross-country flights, including one long XC of 150+ NM with three landing points. You plan, fly, and navigate on your own.

5-10 flight hours
7

Checkride Preparation

Polish skills, review knowledge, and simulate the practical test.

Mock checkrides until you're performing at or above practical test standards. No surprises on test day.

8-12 flight hours
8

Private Pilot Checkride

Oral exam and flight test with an FAA Designated Pilot Examiner.

Demonstrate your knowledge and skills to a DPE. Upon passing — Private Pilot Certificate, earned in a Cirrus SR20.

Instrument Rating (IR)

Fly in clouds, low visibility, and at night with precision. The instrument rating transforms you from a fair-weather pilot into an all-weather aviator — on the G1000 NXi.

40-50 Flight Hours
3-5 Months
Requires PPL
1

Instrument Ground School

IFR regulations, weather theory, approach procedures, and G1000 mastery.

Deep dive into IFR charts, approach plates, ATC communication, weather analysis, and G1000 NXi IFR operations.

30+ hours ground
2

Basic Attitude Instrument Flying

Fly solely by reference to instruments under the hood.

Master straight-and-level, climbs, descents, and turns using the G1000 flight instruments. Build the scan patterns that keep you safe in IMC.

8-10 flight hours
3

Navigation & Instrument Approaches

ILS, VOR, GPS, and RNAV approaches at multiple airports.

Fly precision and non-precision approaches using the G1000 NXi at airports throughout central Alabama.

12-15 flight hours
4

Holding Patterns & Procedures

Master holds, procedure turns, missed approaches, and DME arcs.

The procedural backbone of instrument flying. Proficient holds, missed approaches, and complex arrival procedures.

5-8 flight hours
5

IFR Cross-Country Flights

File and fly real IFR flight plans across the Southeast.

Real-world IFR — filing plans, receiving clearances, flying airways, shooting approaches at unfamiliar airports.

8-10 flight hours
6

Checkride Prep & Instrument Checkride

Mock checkrides, oral prep, and the FAA practical test.

Demonstrate instrument competence to a DPE and earn your instrument rating — unlocking all-weather Cirrus flying.

5-8 hrs + checkride

Commercial Pilot License (CPL)

Turn your passion into a profession. The commercial certificate certifies you to fly for hire — trained entirely in the Cirrus SR20.

50-60 Additional Hours
3-5 Months
Requires PPL + IR
1

Commercial Ground School

Advanced aerodynamics, complex systems, and commercial regulations.

Performance charts, weight and balance, high-altitude operations, and the business of flying.

20+ hours ground

Advanced Maneuvers

Chandelles, lazy eights, steep spirals, and eights on pylons.

Precision maneuvers demonstrating mastery. Smooth, coordinated control at commercial standards.

10-15 flight hours
3

High-Performance Operations

Leverage the SR20's 200HP engine and advanced systems.

Advanced power management, performance calculations, and professional-level systems operation.

5-8 flight hours
4

Time Building & Long Cross-Countries

Build the 250 total hours required for the commercial certificate.

Solo and dual cross-country flights across the Southeast. Includes the required 300nm commercial cross-country.

20-30 flight hours
5

Maneuver Perfection & Stage Checks

Refine every maneuver to commercial pilot standards.

Internal stage checks ensure you're checkride-ready before scheduling the DPE.

8-12 flight hours

Commercial Pilot Checkride

Demonstrate professional-grade skills and earn your CPL.

Pass the oral and flight test — Commercial Pilot Certificate earned in a Cirrus SR20. Welcome to professional aviation.
